================================================================================
                     INTERVIEW INSIGHTS - HOW TO USE THE PROGRAM
================================================================================

WHAT IS THIS PROGRAM?
---------------------
Interview Insights is a tool for recruiters. You enter information about a 
candidate who is going for a client interview, and the program tells you:

  - How risky it is to send this candidate to the client
  - What problems the candidate has
  - What you should do to improve their chances

Think of it as a second opinion before you send someone to a client interview.


================================================================================
                              STEP-BY-STEP TUTORIAL
================================================================================

STEP 1 - OPEN THE APP
----------------------
Open your browser and go to the app URL.

If running locally:   http://localhost:5173
If deployed online:   your Vercel URL (e.g. https://staffinc-task.vercel.app)

You will see a form with the title "Interview Insights".


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 2 - FILL IN CANDIDATE DETAILS
------------------------------------
At the top of the form you will see 4 fields:

  [ Candidate Name ]
  Type the full name of the candidate.
  Example: Alex Johnson

  [ Role Applied For ]
  Type the job title they are interviewing for.
  Example: Senior Software Engineer

  [ Years of Experience ]
  How many years have they worked in this field?
  Example: 4
  (Use 0 if they are a fresh graduate)

  [ Prior Client Rejections ]
  How many times has this candidate been rejected by a client before?
  Example: 0 (if this is their first client interview)
  Example: 2 (if they have been rejected twice before)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 3 - SET THE SCORES
------------------------
You will see 5 sliders. Each slider goes from 0 to 10.
Drag the slider left for a lower score, right for a higher score.

The slider color changes automatically:
  GREEN  = Strong (8 and above)
  YELLOW = Moderate (6 to 7.9)
  RED    = Weak (below 6)

Here is what each score means:

  [ Internal Score ]
  Your agency's overall rating of this candidate.
  How good do YOU think they are overall?
  10 = Excellent candidate
  5  = Average candidate
  1  = Weak candidate

  [ Technical Score ]
  How strong are they technically for this specific role?
  For a software engineer: can they code well?
  For a finance role: do they understand financial concepts?
  10 = Expert level
  5  = Knows the basics
  1  = Significant gaps

  [ Communication Score ]
  How clearly do they express themselves?
  Can they explain things in a way the client will understand?
  10 = Very clear, confident speaker
  5  = Gets the point across but sometimes unclear
  1  = Hard to understand, struggles to explain

  [ Confidence Score ]
  How confident do they come across?
  10 = Very confident, strong presence
  5  = Somewhat confident, a bit hesitant at times
  1  = Very nervous, lacks confidence

  [ Cultural Fit Score ]
  How well would they fit into the client's company culture?
  10 = Perfect fit for the client's environment
  5  = Probably okay, some concerns
  1  = Likely a poor fit


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 4 - WRITE THE QUALITATIVE FEEDBACK
-----------------------------------------
These are two text boxes where you write in your own words.

  [ Recruiter Notes ]
  Write your own observations about the candidate BEFORE the client interview.
  What did you notice during your conversations with them?
  
  Examples of what to write:
  - "Candidate seemed nervous during our prep call"
  - "Very strong technically but struggled to explain things simply"
  - "Great attitude, very enthusiastic about the role"
  - "Hadn't researched the client company at all"
  - "Communication was unclear and rambling at times"

  [ Client Feedback ]
  Write what the CLIENT said after the interview.
  If the interview hasn't happened yet, leave this blank or write "N/A".
  
  Examples of what to write:
  - "Client said the candidate struggled to articulate their experience"
  - "Client felt the candidate was not confident enough for a senior role"
  - "Client mentioned the candidate gave wrong answers on technical questions"
  - "Client said they were unprepared and didn't know much about the company"
  - "Client loved the candidate, very impressed"

  TIP: The more detail you write, the better the analysis will be.
  The program scans your text for specific words and phrases to detect issues.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 5 - CLICK ANALYSE
------------------------
Once you have filled everything in, click the button at the bottom:

  [ Analyse Candidate → ]

The button will show "Analysing..." for a second while it processes.
Then the results will appear automatically.

NOTE: The button will be greyed out if you haven't filled in the 
Candidate Name or Role. Make sure those two fields are filled.


================================================================================
                           READING THE RESULTS
================================================================================

After clicking Analyse, the form disappears and you see the results page.
There are 5 sections:


SECTION 1 - RISK BANNER (top of the page)
------------------------------------------
This is the most important part. It shows:

  RISK LEVEL:
  ✅ LOW      = Strong candidate, good to go with minor preparation
  ⚠️  MEDIUM   = Needs some coaching before the interview
  🔶 HIGH     = Significant issues, needs serious preparation
  🚨 CRITICAL = Do not submit without major work first

  RISK SCORE:
  A number out of 100. The higher the number, the better.
  75-100 = LOW risk
  55-74  = MEDIUM risk
  35-54  = HIGH risk
  0-34   = CRITICAL risk

  The color of the banner matches the risk level:
  Green = LOW, Yellow = MEDIUM, Orange = HIGH, Red = CRITICAL


SECTION 2 - SUMMARY
---------------------
One sentence that summarises the situation.

Example:
"Alex Johnson applying for Senior Software Engineer is a moderate-risk 
candidate who needs targeted coaching (risk score: 61/100). 
3 issues identified across their profile."


SECTION 3 - SCORE BREAKDOWN
-----------------------------
A visual bar chart showing each score dimension.

  - Each bar shows how strong that area is
  - Green bars = strong, Yellow = moderate, Red = weak
  - At the bottom you can see:
    * Weighted total: the combined score before any penalty
    * Rejection penalty: points deducted for previous client rejections
    * Final score: the actual score used to determine risk level

  Why is there a penalty for rejections?
  If a candidate has been rejected by clients before, it increases the risk.
  Each rejection deducts 5 points (maximum 20 points deducted).


SECTION 4 - IDENTIFIED ISSUES
-------------------------------
A list of specific problems detected in this candidate's profile.

Examples:
  ⚡ Below-average communication score
  ⚡ Confidence issues detected in feedback text
  ⚡ Candidate appeared underprepared for the client interview
  ⚡ Candidate has 2 prior client rejections

These come from two sources:
  1. Low scores on the sliders
  2. Negative keywords found in your recruiter notes and client feedback


SECTION 5 - RECOMMENDATIONS
-----------------------------
A numbered list of specific actions you should take.

Examples:
  1. Schedule mock client interviews focusing on structured storytelling 
     (STAR method).
  2. Implement a mandatory pre-interview briefing pack covering the client's 
     business, recent news, and role expectations.
  3. Conduct a structured debrief covering all previous rejections to identify 
     a recurring pattern before any further submissions.

These are tailored to the specific issues found. Each issue gets at least 
one recommendation.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 6 - ANALYSE ANOTHER CANDIDATE
-------------------------------------
At the bottom of the results page, click:

  [ ← Analyse Another Candidate ]

This clears everything and takes you back to the empty form.


================================================================================
                              EXAMPLE WALKTHROUGH
================================================================================

Let's say you have a candidate called Sarah Tan applying for a Project Manager 
role. Here's how you'd fill it in:

  Candidate Name:          Sarah Tan
  Role Applied For:        Project Manager
  Years of Experience:     5
  Prior Client Rejections: 1

  Internal Score:          7.0  (you think she's pretty good)
  Technical Score:         7.5  (solid PM knowledge)
  Communication Score:     5.0  (she tends to ramble)
  Confidence Score:        5.5  (a bit hesitant)
  Cultural Fit Score:      7.0  (should fit the client)

  Recruiter Notes:
  "Sarah is technically strong but during our prep call she was quite nervous 
  and hesitant when answering questions. She also hadn't researched the client 
  company at all."

  Client Feedback:
  "The client said Sarah struggled to articulate her experience clearly and 
  seemed unsure of herself during the interview."

After clicking Analyse, you would likely see:

  Risk Level: HIGH
  Issues: Low communication score, confidence issues, underprepared, 
          prior rejection
  Recommendations: Mock interviews, confidence coaching, briefing pack, 
                   structured debrief on previous rejection


================================================================================
                                TIPS FOR BEST RESULTS
================================================================================

1. Be honest with your scores. If communication is weak, score it low.
   The program can only help if the input is accurate.

2. Write detailed notes. The more you write in the text boxes, the more 
   the program can detect. Short notes like "good candidate" won't trigger 
   any specific insights.

3. Use the client's actual words in the Client Feedback box. 
   Copy-paste from an email if you have one.

4. Always fill in Prior Client Rejections accurately. 
   This has a direct impact on the risk score.

5. Use this BEFORE the client interview too. 
   You don't need client feedback to run an analysis. 
   Use it as a pre-interview check to spot risks early.
