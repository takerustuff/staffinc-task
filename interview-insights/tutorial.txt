================================================================================
                     INTERVIEW INSIGHTS - HOW TO USE THE PROGRAM
================================================================================

WHAT IS THIS PROGRAM?
---------------------
Interview Insights is a tool for recruiters. You enter information about a
candidate who is going for a client interview, and the program tells you:

  - How risky it is to send this candidate to the client
  - What specific problems the candidate has
  - What you should do to improve their chances

Think of it as a second opinion before you send someone to a client interview,
or a structured way to understand why a candidate failed after the fact.


================================================================================
                              STEP-BY-STEP TUTORIAL
================================================================================

STEP 1 - OPEN THE APP
----------------------
Open your browser and go to the app URL.

If running locally:   http://localhost:5173
If deployed online:   your Vercel URL (e.g. https://staffinc-task.vercel.app)

You will see the Interview Insights form.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 2 - SELECT INTERVIEW MODE
--------------------------------
At the top of the form, choose which stage you are analysing:

  [ Pre-Interview ]
  Use this BEFORE the client interview has happened.
  The program will predict the risk of sending this candidate.
  Client Feedback field becomes optional.
  The submit button will say "Predict Risk".

  [ Post-Interview ]
  Use this AFTER the client interview has happened.
  The program will explain why the candidate failed.
  Both recruiter notes and client feedback are scanned for issues.
  The submit button will say "Analyse Failure".

  Which one should I use?
  - Candidate hasn't interviewed yet? Use Pre-Interview.
  - Candidate already interviewed and failed? Use Post-Interview.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 3 - SELECT CLIENT TYPE
-----------------------------
Choose the type of company the candidate is interviewing with.
This changes how the scores are weighted in the analysis.

  [ Corporate ]
  Large, established companies (banks, multinationals, large enterprises).
  Prioritises: Communication and executive presence.
  Communication and confidence are weighted more heavily.

  [ Startup ]
  Early-stage or fast-growing companies.
  Prioritises: Adaptability and initiative.
  Confidence and cultural fit are weighted more heavily.

  [ Consulting ]
  Consulting or professional services firms.
  Prioritises: Structured thinking and polish.
  Technical and communication are both weighted equally high.

  Why does this matter?
  A candidate who is great for a startup might not be right for a corporate
  client. This setting ensures the analysis reflects what the actual client
  values most.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 4 - FILL IN CANDIDATE DETAILS
------------------------------------
  [ Candidate Name ]
  Full name of the candidate.
  Example: Sarah Tan

  [ Role Applied For ]
  The job title they are interviewing for.
  Example: Project Manager

  [ Years of Experience ]
  How many years have they worked in this field?
  Use 0 for fresh graduates.

  [ Prior Client Rejections ]
  How many times has this candidate been rejected by a client before?
  Each rejection adds a penalty to the final risk score (-5 per rejection,
  maximum -20 points).


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 5 - SET THE SCORES
------------------------
There are 5 sliders, each from 0 to 10.
Drag right for a higher score, left for a lower score.

The score value changes color automatically:
  Green  = Strong (8.0 and above)
  Yellow = Moderate (6.0 to 7.9)
  Red    = Weak (below 6.0)

  [ Internal Score ]
  Your agency's overall rating of this candidate.
  How good do YOU think they are overall?

  [ Technical Score ]
  How strong are they technically for this specific role?
  For an engineer: coding ability.
  For a finance role: financial knowledge.
  For a PM: product and process knowledge.

  [ Communication Score ]
  How clearly do they express themselves?
  Can they explain things in a way the client will understand?

  [ Confidence Score ]
  How confident do they come across in conversation?
  Do they speak with authority or do they hesitate?

  [ Cultural Fit Score ]
  How well would they fit into the client's company culture?
  Consider the client type you selected above.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 6 - WRITE THE QUALITATIVE FEEDBACK
-----------------------------------------
These two text boxes are important. The program scans your text for specific
words and phrases to detect issues that scores alone might miss.

  [ Recruiter Notes ]
  Write your own observations about the candidate.
  Be specific and honest. Use the candidate's actual behaviour as examples.

  Good examples:
  - "Candidate seemed nervous during our prep call and second-guessed answers"
  - "Very strong technically but struggled to explain things simply"
  - "Hadn't researched the client company at all before the briefing"
  - "Communication was rambling and hard to follow at times"

  [ Client Feedback ]
  Write what the client said after the interview.
  If using Pre-Interview mode and the interview hasn't happened, leave blank.

  Good examples:
  - "Client said the candidate struggled to articulate their experience"
  - "Client felt the candidate was hesitant and lacked confidence"
  - "Client mentioned the candidate gave incorrect answers on technical topics"
  - "Client said they were unprepared and unaware of the company's products"

  TIP: Copy and paste directly from the client's email if you have one.
  The more specific the language, the better the analysis.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 7 - SUBMIT
----------------
Click the button at the bottom right:

  "Predict Risk"   — if you are in Pre-Interview mode
  "Analyse Failure" — if you are in Post-Interview mode

The button is greyed out if Candidate Name or Role is empty.
It will show "Analysing..." for a moment while the backend processes the data.


================================================================================
                           READING THE RESULTS
================================================================================

After submitting, the form is replaced by the results page.
There are 4 sections:


SECTION 1 - RISK HEADER (top banner)
--------------------------------------
Shows the overall outcome at a glance.

  Mode label: tells you whether this was a Pre or Post interview analysis.

  Risk badge (top right of banner):
    LOW RISK      = Strong candidate, good to go
    MEDIUM RISK   = Needs some coaching before the interview
    HIGH RISK     = Significant issues, needs serious preparation
    CRITICAL RISK = Do not submit without major remediation first

  Risk score (large number):
  A number out of 100. Higher is better.
    75 - 100 = LOW
    55 - 74  = MEDIUM
    35 - 54  = HIGH
    0  - 34  = CRITICAL

  Summary sentence:
  A plain-English description of the candidate's situation, including
  the client type and number of issues found.


SECTION 2 - SCORE BREAKDOWN
-----------------------------
A visual bar chart showing each score dimension.

  Each bar is color-coded:
    Green  = Strong (8+)
    Yellow = Moderate (6-7.9)
    Red    = Weak (below 6)

  At the bottom:
    Weighted total  = combined score before any penalty
    Rejection penalty = points deducted for prior client rejections (if any)
    Final score     = the actual score used to determine risk level

  The weights used depend on the client type you selected:
    Corporate:  Communication 30%, Confidence 25%, Technical 25%
    Startup:    Technical 25%, Confidence 25%, Cultural Fit 20%
    Consulting: Technical 30%, Communication 30%, Confidence 20%


SECTION 3 - IDENTIFIED ISSUES
-------------------------------
A list of specific problems detected in this candidate's profile.

Issues come from two sources:
  1. Low scores on the sliders (below threshold for the client type)
  2. Negative keywords found in recruiter notes and client feedback

Examples of issues you might see:
  - Below-average communication score
  - Confidence issues detected in feedback
  - Candidate appeared underprepared for the client interview
  - Cultural fit below startup threshold
  - Candidate has 2 prior client rejections


SECTION 4 - RECOMMENDATIONS
-----------------------------
A numbered list of specific actions tailored to the issues found.
Recommendations are also adjusted based on the client type.

Examples:
  Corporate client with communication issue:
  "For corporate clients, executive presence is critical — coach the candidate
  on formal communication, structured answers, and boardroom-ready delivery."

  Startup client with confidence issue:
  "Startups want self-starters. Coach the candidate to demonstrate initiative
  and comfort with ambiguity."

  Preparation issue (any client type):
  "Implement a mandatory pre-interview briefing pack covering the client's
  business, recent news, and role expectations."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

BACK TO FORM
-------------
At the bottom of the results page, click "Back to Form" to clear everything
and analyse another candidate.


================================================================================
                              EXAMPLE WALKTHROUGH
================================================================================

Scenario:
Sarah Tan is applying for a Project Manager role at a large corporate bank.
She has 5 years of experience. She had one previous client rejection.
You are doing a pre-interview check before sending her.

Fill in the form like this:

  Interview Mode:          Pre-Interview
  Client Type:             Corporate
  Candidate Name:          Sarah Tan
  Role Applied For:        Project Manager
  Years of Experience:     5
  Prior Client Rejections: 1

  Internal Score:          7.0
  Technical Score:         7.5
  Communication Score:     5.0  (she tends to ramble)
  Confidence Score:        5.5  (a bit hesitant)
  Cultural Fit Score:      7.0

  Recruiter Notes:
  "Sarah is technically strong but during our prep call she was quite nervous
  and hesitant when answering questions. She also hadn't researched the client
  company at all."

  Client Feedback:
  (leave blank — interview hasn't happened yet)

After clicking "Predict Risk", you would see:

  Risk Level:   HIGH
  Risk Score:   ~52/100
  Issues:       Low communication score, confidence issues, underprepared,
                prior rejection penalty applied
  Recommendations:
    1. For corporate clients, executive presence is critical — coach on formal
       communication and boardroom-ready delivery.
    2. Corporate clients expect authority and decisiveness. Run mock interviews
       with a senior stakeholder to build executive presence.
    3. Implement a mandatory pre-interview briefing pack covering the client's
       business, recent news, and role expectations.
    4. Conduct a structured debrief on the previous rejection to identify
       recurring patterns.

Action: Do not submit Sarah yet. Run coaching sessions first, then re-assess.


================================================================================
                              TIPS FOR BEST RESULTS
================================================================================

1. Be honest with your scores.
   If communication is weak, score it low. The program can only help if the
   input is accurate.

2. Write detailed notes.
   Short notes like "good candidate" won't trigger any specific insights.
   The more specific your language, the more the program can detect.

3. Use the client's actual words in the Client Feedback box.
   Copy-paste from an email if you have one.

4. Always set the correct Client Type.
   A score of 6.5 on communication is fine for a startup but may be flagged
   as a risk for a corporate client.

5. Use Pre-Interview mode proactively.
   Don't wait for a rejection to use this tool. Run it before every submission
   to catch risks early.

6. Prior Client Rejections matters.
   Even a strong candidate with 2+ rejections will have their score penalised.
   Use the Post-Interview mode after each rejection to understand the pattern.

REJECTION PENALTY:
  1 rejection  = -5 points
  2 rejections = -10 points
  3 rejections = -15 points
  4+ rejections = -20 points (maximum)

KEYWORDS THE PROGRAM DETECTS IN TEXT:
  Communication: "unclear", "rambling", "struggled to articulate", "hard to follow"
  Confidence:    "nervous", "hesitant", "unsure", "timid", "lacked confidence"
  Technical:     "wrong answer", "gaps in knowledge", "couldn't solve", "incorrect"
  Cultural Fit:  "not a fit", "arrogant", "unprofessional", "disengaged"
  Preparation:   "unprepared", "didn't research", "unaware of", "not familiar with"
